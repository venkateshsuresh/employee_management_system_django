AUGMENTED REALITY BASED PRODUCT ASSEMBLY

Project Overview

There is a huge market base in India for all kinds of industries. The revolution of Digital India gives us a great platform to incorporate emerging technologies in industries. In the current industrial trends, thousands of products are being manufactured and assembled every day. Consumer specific and customized products, as well as increased product complexity, set higher demands for assembly work. A typical assembly process involves grouping of individual parts to form a single unit. Pre-training is required for the operators before getting into the work. The recent articles of a leading newspaper in the country points out that nearly every type of assembling sector faces increasing regulations aimed at everything right from ensuring product safety to time and speed management. Also, product preferences change so quickly that delays in assembling and production may be a death knell for the industry. Every new product right from manufacturing smartphones to heavy machinery equipment requires a different set of assembling instructions. Additional trainers for operators increase labour cost. There is a shortage of skilled operators and a lack of knowledge about the assembly line process due to which the companies experience unnecessary buffers that negatively impact the bottom line. “The Indian Institute of Welding has estimated a short supply of 1.2 million welding professionals”. Not only welding but also there is a lack of many other professional workers in the assembly sector. Every industry expects to see rapid growth and value addition. Update, work speed, and time consumption are the 3 most pre-dominant key factors in the industrial market that help the industries achieve their goal. Many manufacturing companies are developing robust training programs to teach their candidates right from welding to complex assembly-line process. Nearly 4.6 million jobs will become available over the next decade and nearly 2.4 million are expected to go unfilled. Today 6 out of 10 open skilled assembling and production positions are unfilled. While automation and robotics may help to fill the labour gap, skilled manual workers will still be needed in the assembling process, perform analysis and manage production. The main objective of the solution is to reduce the complexity of assembly work by developing a wearable device using Augmented Reality technology that can overcome the above-stated challenge faced by industries in the present scenario, where time and speed of the work plays a significant role. AR can help by overlaying audio-visual 3-D model procedural instructions and contextual information generated with graphics, text, and sound directly over the image target for assembling a product that strikes out the need of an expert in the assembly unit. From the study analysis and evaluation of existing solutions, all the information is in the form of 2-D contents and smart glasses subjects for a huge cost.

Introduction

The assembly phase is an important process during the product development cycle. Currently, manual operation is still the main style for some non-automated or semi-automated production lines even in the era of Industry 4.0. Assembly operations usually are carried out in many steps defined by process engineer in an assembly manual, where each step involves different assembly objects, tools and quality requirements that should be paid attention. Manual assembly performed by human workers results in the diversity of assembly time, quality, and cost. In the mass custom production condition, the frequently changed product and their assembly process require that the skilled and qualified workers should keep in mind all the trivial process when they assembly the product. The assembly error will turn up higher because of the heavy cognitive load. Traditionally, PAM is used in industries with extensive text and guiding pictures. The text and 2D diagrams are difficult to understand the complex assembly relations, which results in a long time for training and execution. The 3D virtual reality assembly manual has also been the main method to assistant the assembly works. The VAM can provide dynamic and visual assembly instruction to workers and aid the understanding of the spatial relationship, sequence, and path during the assembling process. But the VAM separates the real assembly work environments from the virtual information, which require the attention transfer between virtual and real assembly environment. In the era of Industry 4.0, more and more work support systems will be provided to assistant workers. Augmented reality applications in product assembly can notably improve the operation efficiency of shop floor workers in training or onsite work. The assembly manual system based on augmented reality technology is more complex than manuals in and virtual three-dimensional manual. This research work introduces a general two stages procedure for application of augmented assembly manual system. The relationship of assembly activities is described and the IDEF0 function diagram for augmented assembly application is presented. A detailed system framework for augmented assembly manual application is proposed and two stand-alone experimental systems with file transfer mechanisms are developed to instruct and assistant labours in assembly work at shop floor workstations.

Working Principle and Methodology

Augmented Reality uses existing reality and physical objects to trigger computer-generated enhancements over the top of reality, in the real image on a user’s view of the real world, thus providing a composite view. These images can be of 3D models, videos and information. The goal of AR is to create a system in which the user cannot differentiate the real world and virtual augmentation of it. AR adds digital elements onto a smartphone camera, creating an illusion that holographic content is a part of the physical world around you. In contrast with VR, you are not immersed in the whole artificial environment. AR alters the surroundings a bit by adding 3D objects, sounds, videos, graphics to it. To show the relevant content to the user AR uses computer vision, simultaneous localization, mapping and depth tracking. This allows cameras to collect, send and process data to show digital content relevant to what the user is looking at. The whole process of computer seeing real world includes the machine representing colours by numbers, identifying a similar group of colours and then segmenting the image, searching for lines that meet at object angles and covering a specific part of the image, finding textures, and matching the image with those present in the database. Fig 3.1, shows the general requirements. Augmented reality requires discerning objects around the user in terms of both semantics and 3D geometry. Semantics recognizes the object, while geometry figures out where the object is placed.

Software Description

Maya is a 3-D computer animation, modelling, simulation, and rendering software. It is developed by Autodesk that enables the creation of high professional three-dimensional structures of objects. In the proposed solution, the use of Maya has resulted in the creation of the replica of the industrial product parts and individual components of the machinery product in 3D with greater precision. The developed 3D models are imported in the Unity 3D gaming engine software. This approach applies to any other parts 3D model’s equipment. Using the Autodesk Maya 3D software tool, the models are developed and imported in unity for transformations. The application consists of several object’s assembly instructions which have a different kind of components. Each assembly instruction has several steps that have an animation applied to the 3D model which is created using Maya.

UNITY

The main part of AR is carried in Unity 3D. Unity 3D engine is the popular development platform for creating 2D and 3D, augmented reality multiplatform interactive experiences. Unity engine integrated with MAYA 3D is used here. Using C# code, the animation and simulation are done in unity. This application developed is intended for android users. This project is Marker-based Augmented Reality. It is a powerful cross-platform 3D engine and also a user-friendly environment. It is used to create applications for mobile, desktop, and web. In our solution, unity software is used to develop the marker-based smart AR application which can be viewed with the help of the smart glass designed. The application aims at delivering visual information features, function and operation are effectively defined.

Vuforia

It is a software development kit (SDK) specially used for AR. It is used in devices that enable the creation of AR applications. The mosaicking and fusion of common parts of the different images support us for locating and placing the degree of the viewpoint of virtual object models created in 3-D and synthetic images which gives the real view similar to the real-world environment when viewed through a mobile camera. The continuous tracking of virtual objects helps us to match the location and viewpoint angle if the image is very realistic. The 3-D virtual object looks very real in the viewer's perspective and changes to the closeness of the vision for the target object present in the 3-D environment for providing real-world experience. Vuforia 3-D engine is a platform for creating Augmented Reality applications. It allows to recognize image target and enables to interact with spaces in the real world. This software uses the capabilities of the computer vision technology to recognize and make the individual tracking of the objects captured by the camera in real-time. Vuforia capable to do image registration which offers the developers to change the position and the orientation of the virtual object in the space, mainly 3D objects or another type of media, concerning the real-life environment or video when these are viewed through the smartphone’s camera. The virtual object or objects appears as if from another real live environment object. The Vuforia SDK supports different types of targets, both two-dimensional and three-dimensional, including multi-target configurations, user-defined image targets and frame markers. The SDK also provides another additional feature such as localized occlusion detections using virtual buttons, image target selection in real-time and the ability to reconfigure and create target sets depending on the scenario. There are three main components to the Vuforia platform: The Vuforia Engine is the client-side library that is statically linked to the app. This is available through the client SDK and supports Android and iOS. Tools Vuforia provides tools for creating the target marker, managing the target database and securing the licenses of the application.
